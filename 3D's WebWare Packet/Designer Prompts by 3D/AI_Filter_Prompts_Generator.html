How To Use This Generator


      After you click Start, You will be given options to pick from. You slowly design your prompt by handling one point of the imagery at a time. When you are almost done, you will be presented with a settings page that will allow you to tweek your prompt a bit more before generation.
      Once ready to continue, You will be asked to review your prompt generalized text before continuing to generating your prompt(s).


      At the bottom of every option page is slightly more information about each option currently on screen.


      You cannot go backward once beginning so you will have to refresh the page if a messup is made so read over your inputs closely or use the presets.


      You get 3 free daily prompt builds (no questions asked). You can get a limited version license for about 8$ USD/BTC (during beta), which is unlocked prompt generating. That paid version can be downloaded, stored and ran locally and should always work once running via file.


      At the bottom of every option page is slightly more information about each option currently on screen.




Try the Rainbow Bridge


      Let the path the colors present for you decide your next move to ensure a safe journey through the prompt genreation process. Nordish refrences aside, there is a colored line that adjusts itself based on your prompt design actions.
      Every preset has a color underneath to show optional assistance throughout the prompt design process. Howevery, If you are unsure of which setting or preset may be best suited for your build, use your Build HUD Rainbow Bridge to help guide you.
      Your guiding light or Rainbow bridge (line) will appear just below your Build HUD so if you pick a color near your guiding light you should get a good output.

      Don't worry if your guiding light does or doesn't change colors after picking an option. The guiding light is completely based on your actions and will always be guiding your needed next move. If you are still unsure which colors are right for you, try finding a gold, yellow, white or green colored option for these four colors tend to work for most prompt builds.
      Try using various color combinations to create more unique color end-outputs for more uniquely custom designs.
      If you end up with a broken bridge (black or invisible guiding light) then just click Random to finish the series for a fun time!

Standard Portals

  Portal: An opening within two different spaces that are inequivalent to the spaces on either side.

  Portal (Game Ref): An opening within two different spaces that may be inequivalent to the spaces on either side. May produce externally imaged glow reflections.

  Hole: An opening within two different spaces that are inequivalent to the spaces on either side but the hole is of the spaces form their side.

  Eyes: An opening within two different spaces. One side is inequivalent to the space on either side and one side is viewed from within an eye. May misshappen outputs to mimic rounded edges as if being seen within a rounded shape (like an eye ball).




Old Alternative Portals

  Door: An opening within two different spaces. One side is inequivalent to the space around it. May produce edging and framing around image (considered a rare output).

  Window: An opening within two different spaces. One side is inequivalent to the space around it. May produce edging and framing around image (considered an uncommon output).   Between Space: An opening within a singular host space. Client side is inequivalent to the host space.

  God-View: A form of viewing from the sky downward onto the subjects. Not intended to give a top-down view but is intended for 'High in Sky' type of view.

 Tips 

 1a)  Adding "," to the end of a prompt when knowing other similar (positive or negative) prompts will follow will help combine prompts

 1b)  Adding "." to the end of a prompt when knowing other similar (positive or negative) prompts will follow will help separate prompts

 1c)  Adding ";" after a word set in a prompt can help separate logic comparisons during early stages of generating images

 1d)  Having negative prompts after a set of positive prompts will apply the negative offsets to the previous set of prompt considerations during logic comparisons

 2a)  The 1st prompt will always be the "base" prompt and the last prompt will always be the "last edited" during generation. In Example: if 1st prompt set describes general image scene, that will be the 1st generated set of objects, the next set of prompts will apply to each other in sequential order so then the last prompt set will be the most visible changes from the first prompt set

 2b)  Base Prompts should contain (order of importance, 1st said most important): Imagery of image, Main Visual object(s), details, imagery considerations, additional objects, effects, sizing of image (resolution)

 3)  Sizing of image (resolution) is not the Aspect Ratio but the amount of nit/pixel/niche/groups within the image being generated. So "16k Resolution 32 Megapixel" is going to have about 16k pixels horizontal but contain only a soft approximate maximum of 32 million pixels for the entire image. Considering how many pixels you need for the number of pixels in a line you want will give sharper or more blurry images. Prompts like, "Radial Centered Pixel Cluster {6 megapixels}", can help direct sharpness to sections of the expected end image.

 4)  The words used can make a general or specific description that will act as either general or specific commands. The more specific you are, the more illustrations and potential training models needed for generating as expected results. The more general you are, the more the AI has to make its own decisions when redrawing after the disruption phases (aka "ripping") of the generating process. A good example is being specific on how many pixels you want in total may produce more blurry results or 'not in focus' results (compared to other sections of same output) but not being specific may produce more overall blurry images or more overall in-focus (not sharp) images.

 5a)  Ending a prompt series (stacked prompts) with a negative weight can cause over baking (digitalization in output, static in output, extremely malformed imagery), reverse saturation (of imagery not just coloring) or the negative prompt will be applied as a positive prompt and add some of these specific terms as needed imagery. When this happens, change the seed or add a specific numerical seed.

 5b)  Not ending a prompt series with a negative weight can cause over baking and over saturation of imagery for last prompt while disregarding first prompt. When this happens, change the seed or add a specific numerical seed.

 6a)  Feed backing is giving the AI a previous output as an input during same session.

 6b)  Backtracking is giving the AI a previous output of not the same session or AI/AI Core Provider.

 6c)  Feed backing/Backtracking without changing anything (other than prompt) can create an over baking avalanche (won't stop over baking with each new output). Change the Model version used then change back (if needed).

 6d)  Good Feed backing for better details: (your output) --> 1.5 @K_DP MPP_2S_ANCESTRAL (or any ancestral) --> 2.1 auto --> 1.5 auto (or @K_LMS)

 7)  Use Noise to change things up sometimes if the AI keeps doing "the opposite" of what you are wanting or start back from scratch (where your start was).
